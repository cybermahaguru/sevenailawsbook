# Code of Conduct

## Our standard, in one sentence

**Argue hard, attack ideas, respect people** — the same rules a good moot court would enforce.

---

## The long version

This project exists to advance public debate on the governance of artificial intelligence. That debate is impossible if contributors fear personal attack, harassment, or exclusion based on who they are rather than what they argue.

We are therefore committed to making participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, caste, or sexual identity and orientation.

---

## Expected behaviour

- **Cite, don't accuse.** Disagreement is welcome; disparagement is not.
- **Steelman opposing views** before knocking them down. *Mill, On Liberty (1859)*: he who knows only his own side of the case knows little of that.
- **Assume good faith** until evidence forces otherwise.
- **Be patient with newcomers**, including those whose first language is not English.
- **Credit prior work**, including translators, annotators, and reviewers.

---

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Personal attacks, insults, or slurs.
- Harassment, public or private — including unwanted contact after a clear request to stop.
- Sexualised language or imagery in any project space.
- Doxxing — publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, Issues, or PRs.
- Advocacy of, or encouragement of, any of the above.

---

## Enforcement

The project maintainer is responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code.

**To report a violation**, email **cyberlawconsulting@gmail.com** with subject line `[CoC] <brief description>`. Reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The maintainer is obliged to respect the privacy and security of the reporter.

Consequences may include:

1. **Correction** — a private written warning, clarifying the violation.
2. **Warning** — a public or private warning with consequences for continued behaviour.
3. **Temporary ban** — from any interaction or public communication with the project for a specified period.
4. **Permanent ban** — for sustained or egregious violations.

---

## Scope

This Code applies within all project spaces — Issues, PRs, Discussions, the linked Internet Archive page, and any official social-media channel — and when an individual is representing the project in public.
