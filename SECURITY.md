# Security & Sensitive-Issue Disclosure Policy

This repository hosts a book, not a software product. "Security" here therefore covers four narrower categories:

1. **Vulnerabilities in companion scripts or website code** (if any are added later)
2. **Privacy / personal-data exposure** in commits, Issues, or PRs
3. **DMCA / copyright / attribution disputes**
4. **Plagiarism or misuse of the work** in violation of CC-BY 4.0

---

## How to report

**Please do NOT open a public Issue for any of the above.**

Email **cyberlawconsulting@gmail.com** with subject line:

- `[SECURITY] <brief description>` — for code or privacy issues
- `[DMCA] <brief description>` — for copyright disputes
- `[ATTRIBUTION] <brief description>` — for missing or incorrect credit

Include:

- A clear description of the issue
- Your contact details (and whether you wish to be credited or remain anonymous)
- For DMCA notices: the statutory elements required under 17 U.S.C. § 512(c)(3) or the equivalent under the **Indian Information Technology Act, 2000** and the **Copyright Act, 1957, s. 52(1)(c)**.
- For attribution disputes: links to your work, the alleged reuse, and the proposed remedy

These are targets, not contractual commitments — this is a single-author open-access project.

---

## Safe-harbour for good-faith reports

Reporters acting in good faith will not face legal action from the author for disclosure, even if the report turns out to be mistaken. This follows the spirit of *Van Buren v. United States*, 593 U.S. 374 (2021), which narrowed the criminalisation of good-faith inquiry under the U.S. Computer Fraud and Abuse Act.

---

## What is *not* a security issue

- Disagreement with the Doctrines or the Seven Laws — open a public Issue labelled `dissent`.
- Citation corrections — open a public Issue or PR.
- Translation requests — open a public Issue.
- Feature requests for companion materials — open a public Issue.
